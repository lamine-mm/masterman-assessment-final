# Masterman Voice

## When to use
Any time you write user-facing copy: landing page, questions, result page, emails, error messages, button labels, loading states. Everything.

## Core voice
Shaykh Abdullah Oduro's voice — imam-as-older-brother. Direct. Warm. Grounded in Qur'an and Sunnah. Never motivational-speaker. Never clinical.

## Rules

### Language
- Grade 5 reading level. Short sentences. Common words.
- Arabic Islamic terms are welcome when they carry specific meaning: nafs, muhasaba, du'a, barakah, in shaa Allah, Alhamdulillah. Do not italicize them in copy.
- "Allah" not "God" in user-facing copy.

### Forbidden vocabulary
- "Mindset," "level up," "crush it," "best self," "unleash," "hack," "optimize your life," "high performer," "10x," "alpha"
- "Spiritual journey" (too vague), "authentic self" (secular), "find yourself" (secular)
- Therapy-speak: "trauma response," "inner child," "attachment style," "triggered"
- Hype: "life-changing," "transformational," "game-changer"

### Preferred vocabulary
- "The man you were made to be"
- "What Allah put in you"
- "The gap between who you are and who you are supposed to be"
- "Lead yourself first"
- "Your house," "your brothers," "your wife," "your children"
- "Stand up," "sit with it," "return to," "answer to"

### Tone
- Brotherly, not professorial
- Honest, not harsh
- Invites reflection, does not lecture
- Names the struggle directly ("most men live here")

## Examples

**Bad:** *"Unlock your full potential as a spiritual leader in your household."*
**Good:** *"Lead your house the way Allah asked you to."*

**Bad:** *"This personality assessment will help you discover your authentic self."*
**Good:** *"This will show you where you actually stand — not where you tell people you stand."*

**Bad:** *"Congrats! You're a high-performing ENTJ archetype."*
**Good:** *"You are the Anchored Shepherd. Your house runs toward you, not away from you."*

**Bad:** *"Click here to begin your transformation journey."*
**Good:** *"Take 4 minutes. Answer honestly. See where you are."*

## Question writing
- Direct second person: "I know why Allah put me on this earth." Not "The user understands their purpose."
- Concrete, not abstract: "My phone controls more of my day than I control of it." Not "I struggle with digital discipline."
- Honest, not flattering: written so the wrong answer feels obvious to a man lying to himself.

## Result page writing
- Type name is a title of identity, not a label of personality
- Strengths line names one thing, not three
- Blind spot line names the nafs trap specifically
- Qur'an/Sunnah anchor is one line, pulled from the approved content file — never invented

## When in doubt
Ask: "Would Shaykh Abdullah say this to his own younger brother over tea?" If no, rewrite.
